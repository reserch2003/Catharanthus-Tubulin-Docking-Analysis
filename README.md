# Catharanthus-Tubulin-Docking-Analysis
Comparative molecular docking analysis of Catharanthus roseus alkaloids against tubulin protein (1SA0) using CB-Dock2 and PyMOL.

# Comparative Molecular Docking Analysis of Catharanthus roseus Alkaloids Against Tubulin Protein (1SA0)

## Objective
This project focuses on the molecular docking analysis of selected alkaloids from Catharanthus roseus against tubulin protein (1SA0) to evaluate their potential anticancer interactions.

## Ligands Used
- Catharanthine
- Ajmalicine
- Vindoline

## Target Protein
- Tubulin Protein (PDB ID: 1SA0)

## Tools and Databases
- CB-Dock2
- PyMOL
- PubChem
- RCSB PDB

## Methodology
1. Protein structure was obtained from RCSB PDB.
2. Ligand structures were downloaded from PubChem.
3. Molecular docking was performed using CB-Dock2.
4. Docked complexes were visualized in PyMOL.
5. Binding affinities were compared for analysis.

## Results

| Ligand | Best Binding Affinity (kcal/mol) |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Catharanthine | -9.0 |
| Ajmalicine | -8.3 |
| Vindoline | -8.0 |

## Conclusion
Among the selected ligands, Catharanthine exhibited the strongest binding affinity toward tubulin protein, indicating promising interaction potential.

## Future Scope
Further studies involving molecular dynamics simulations and ADMET analysis may provide deeper insight into the therapeutic potential of these alkaloids.
